# 1. Industry Overview

Backflow prevention testing is a specialized plumbing service that ensures water supply systems remain uncontaminated by preventing water from flowing backwards into public water supplies. This industry serves both commercial and residential customers, with commercial properties being the primary revenue drivers.

The industry addresses critical public health concerns by testing backflow prevention devices that protect municipal water systems from contamination. According to the American Water Works Association (AWWA), backflow incidents can introduce chemicals, bacteria, and other contaminants into drinking water supplies, making regular testing mandatory in most jurisdictions.

The customer base consists primarily of:
- Commercial property owners (restaurants, hospitals, manufacturing facilities)
- Municipal water utilities
- Property management companies
- Industrial facilities with cross-connections
- Multi-family residential complexes

This is a regulatory-driven service with consistent demand created by mandatory annual testing requirements established by the Safe Drinking Water Act and enforced at the local level through water utility departments.

# 2. Customer Problem & Demand

The core problem customers face is maintaining compliance with mandatory backflow prevention testing requirements while avoiding water service disconnection, fines, and potential liability for water contamination incidents.

**Urgency Level**: High - Most jurisdictions require annual testing with strict deadlines. Non-compliance typically results in water service termination notices, making this an urgent operational necessity.

**Consequences of Non-Action**:
- Water service disconnection (business shutdown)
- Municipal fines ranging from $200-$500+ per day
- Potential liability for contamination incidents
- Failed health department inspections
- Insurance claim complications

**Why Internal Handling Isn't Viable**:
- Requires specialized certification and training
- Testing equipment costs $3,000-$8,000 per unit
- Limited usage frequency doesn't justify internal investment
- Regulatory liability requires professional expertise and insurance coverage

The demand is operationally mandatory rather than discretionary - businesses cannot operate without water service, and water service requires current backflow testing certification.
