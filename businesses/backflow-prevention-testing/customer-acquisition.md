## Objection Handling

### Objection 1: "We already have someone for that."
**What it means:** They have an existing vendor relationship  
**Response:** "That's great — having a reliable tester is important. Just curious, are you happy with their scheduling flexibility and turnaround time? The reason I ask is we often help as a backup when primary vendors get booked up in December."

### Objection 2: "Not in the budget right now."
**What it means:** They don't see the value or priority  
**Response:** "I understand budget constraints. Just so you know, Minneapolis violations start at $500 per day, plus potential liability issues. Our testing typically costs less than one day's violation fee. Is this something you budget for annually, or does it come from maintenance funds?"

### Objection 3: "Send me some information."
**What it means:** Polite brush-off, not ready to engage  
**Response:** "I could send you a generic brochure, but that won't help you much. Let me ask — what specific information would be most helpful? Your testing schedule, pricing, or something else? I can give you exactly what you need in 5 minutes on the phone."

### Objection 4: "How are you different from other testers?"
**What it means:** They want to understand your unique value  
**Response:** "Good question. Three things: First, we handle all the city paperwork and follow-up — most testers just hand you the results. Second, we offer emergency testing within 24 hours if you fail an inspection. Third, we're local — I can be at your facility in 30 minutes if there's an issue."
